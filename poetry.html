ODE TO POULTRY

In verdant fields bathed by golden sun rays,
Strut the chickens, in a feathered ballet.
They peck and they squawk, with a glorious fuss,
Scratching for snacks in the dew-kissed grass.

Their beaks like tiny shovels, they dig and they delve,
Uncovering treasures, a worm or a weevil.
But beneath those fluffy wings, a secret they keep,
A truth far juicier, a taste oh-so-deep.

For chicken, my friends, though they strut and they crow,
Hold a destiny tastier than anyone knows.
Roasted to golden, with crispy, brown skin,
A succulent symphony, a culinary sin.

So next time you see them, these feathered buffoons,
Remember the drumsticks, the wings, and the moons
Of meaty delight, waiting just out of reach,
A secret far greater than any bird-brained screech.

So raise a drumstick, a thigh, or a wing,
To the clucking, the strutting, the tasty, the king,
Of barnyard bounty, the feathered delight,
The chicken, oh chicken, just perfect for bite!
